Beth Chatto’s Plants and Gardens
Covering six glorious acres, the Beth Chatto Gardens were established in 1960 on an overgrown wasteland, and have become world famous for their beauty and innovation. Explore this informal garden that harmonises with the surrounding East Anglian countryside, and marvel at the world-famous drought-resistant Gravel Garden.

Belmont House and Gardens in Faversham
Visit Belmont House, an elegant 18th-century house designed by Samuel Wyatt. This is one of the most prestigious houses in Kent and is steeped in history. Explore the 3,000-acre grounds which boasts lovely views of the Kentish North Downs, formal gardens, a cricket pitch, orchards, farmland and woodland.

Parham House and gardens
Explore the grandeur of Parham House, which dates to 1577. Set in the heart of a medieval deer park at the foot of the South Downs, Parham’s four-acre Walled Garden is a tapestry of colour and rich scents and is surrounded by the seven-acre Pleasure Grounds, which feature a lake nestled among trees and native heathers, intricated turf and a brick maze.

Trebah Gardens
Trebah is a wooded 26-acre sub-tropical ravine garden that descends to its own beach on the beautiful Helford River. Steeped in history, this beautiful garden is the result of over 180 years of inspiration and dedication. Explore four miles of footpaths that wind through dazzling exotic planting, transporting you from this little corner of England to sunnier climes.
